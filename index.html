About Me

A finance-first builder with a global lens

I'm a bilingual, bicultural finance professional passionate about using data, technology, and strategy to solve complex business problems. After earning a B.S. in Business Administration from Northeastern University — concentrating in Finance and FinTech — and studying International Business Management at CEFAM in Lyon, France, I'm now pursuing a Master of Finance at MIT Sloan School of Management, deepening my expertise in finance, machine learning, and financial innovation.

My experience spans consulting, investment analysis, and business transformation — including roles at Boston Consulting Group as an Investment Analyst Co-op and at Ernst & Young in Business Transformation, alongside work as a Research Analyst, an Investment Analyst with NUImpact, a Research Lead with UNICEF, and a Co-op Peer Mentor guiding fellow students through their career journeys.

I thrive at the intersection of finance, AI, and emerging technologies, with a long-term ambition to build fintech solutions that expand financial inclusion and drive strategic transformation across global — and especially emerging — markets.

My Journey

From Abidjan to Cambridge

Rooted in Côte d'Ivoire

I left Abidjan at 18 with one dream: studying in the United States. That decision — and the culture, resilience, and "village" that raised me in Côte d'Ivoire — still shapes how I think about finance today: as a tool for opportunity, not just optimization.

I stay closely connected to home, from engaging with the Côte d'Ivoire Business Club to running independent research on financial behavior in Abidjan — because I believe emerging markets, and West Africa in particular, are where some of the most interesting fintech problems are waiting to be solved.

Field Research
